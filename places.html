BUABO-BUABO

Museo San Nicoleno, also known as Buabo-Buabo, isn't just famous for its awesome collection, it's super cool because it gets the whole community involved in showing off our local heritage. The Local students become tour guides, learning all about our art, especially the Damili pottery. These student guides are like the VIP key to understanding our history, connecting tourists to the heart of our culture.

DIOCESAN SHRINE AND PARISH OF SAN NICOLAS

Originally built in 1584, along with the founding of the town by Augustinian friars, San Nicolas Church has always been special. The town's first name, Visita de Caluntian, came from the many lanuti trees around. Father Antonio Villanueva gave the church a major upgrade in 1693. And get this on September 10, 2020, it was officially recognized as a diocesan shrine, thanks to Bishop Renato Mayugba's approval on July 21, 2020.

ROBINSONS ILOCOS

Robinsons Place Ilocos, which opened on December 3, 2009, is the first full-service mall in Ilocos Norte. Since its launch, it has become a central hub for shopping, dining, and entertainment in the region. Due to its strong performance and growing popularity, Robinsons Land Corporation expanded the mall in 2016 with the addition of a south wing. This expansion introduced more retail and dining options, as well as a BPO office component, catering to the increasing demand from both the local Ilocano community and tourists.

BALAY SAN NICOLAS

A well-preserved ancestral house located in the town of San Nicolas, Ilocos Norte, Philippines. It serves as a symbol of the town's rich cultural heritage and history. Believed to be over a century old, the structure showcases traditional Ilocano-Spanish colonial architecture, featuring capiz shell windows, hardwood floors, and intricate woodwork that reflect the craftsmanship of the era. Originally a private residence, Balay San Nicolas has since been repurposed as a cultural and historical center. It now houses exhibits and memorabilia that highlight the town'Ss history, notable personalities, and contributions to the province. The house also serves as a venue for cultural events, local celebrations, and educational tours, helping to preserve and promote the heritage of San Nicolas for future generations.
